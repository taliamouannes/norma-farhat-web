About

Norma Farhat is a Lebanese-French painter, born in 1972 in Beirut. After studying at the Canadian school Mission Renaissance in Beirut, she specialized in oil painting on canvas.

She mainly creates landscapes and portraits, strongly influenced by the Impressionist period. In 2013, she founded her own painting school, L’Atelier Monot, in Beirut, where she taught drawing, tone and color studies.

She teaches various artistic techniques: charcoal, pastel, gouache, watercolor, oil painting, as well as reproductions of works by great Impressionist and classical masters.

Following the Covid crisis in 2019 and the Beirut port explosion, she moved to Paris in 2020 and continued her work as an independent teacher.
